Hyderabadi Biryani

Home

Description

Hyderabadi Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful rice dish originating from the city of Hyderabad, India. It is made with basmati rice, marinated meat (usually chicken or mutton), and a blend of spices. The dish is known for its rich aroma and taste, often cooked using the dum method where the ingredients are slow-cooked together to enhance the flavors.

Ingredients

  • 500g basmati rice
  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 4-5 whole cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 tablespoons ghee or oil

Steps

  1. Wash and soak the basmati rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large bowl, marinate the chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, mint leaves, coriander leaves, garam masala, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour.
  3. In a heavy-bottomed pot or pressure cooker, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add the whole spices (cloves, cardamom pods, bay leaves, cumin seeds) and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  6. Add the marinated chicken to the pot and cook until the chicken is browned and cooked through.
  7. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and add the soaked rice along with some salt. Cook the rice until it is about 70% done (it should still have a bite).
  8. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken in the pot. Cover it tightly with a lid or aluminum foil to trap the steam.
  9. Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes (dum cooking) to allow the flavors to meld together.
  10. Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork and serve hot with raita or salad.
