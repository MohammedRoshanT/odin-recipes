Chicken Shawarma

Home

Description

Chicken Shawarma is a popular Middle Eastern dish made by marinating chicken in a blend of spices and herbs, then slow-cooking it on a vertical rotisserie. The result is tender, flavorful chicken that is typically served in pita bread with fresh vegetables and sauces. It's a delicious and satisfying meal that can be enjoyed as street food or at home.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken thighs, boneless and skinless
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix yogurt, lemon juice, oil, garlic, ginger, cumin, coriander, garam masala, paprika, and salt to create the marinade.
  2. Add the chicken thighs to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight for best results.
  3. Preheat your grill or oven to a high temperature (around 200°C/400°F).
  4. Remove the chicken from the marinade and place it on the grill or in a baking dish.
  5. Cook for about 20-25 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and has a nice char.
  6. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before slicing it thinly.
  7. Serve in pita bread with fresh vegetables like lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, and your choice of sauces such as tahini or garlic sauce.
